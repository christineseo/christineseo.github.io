Barbarian Redesign

Redesigning Barbarian's Website



How can we redesign the current Barbarian website?

In summer 2019 I worked as an UI/UX intern at Barbarian, a creative technology company creates engaging content for advertising. My main project was enhancing the Barbarian website that expresses the company’s works and values.

Summer 2019: UX Internship
Team: Sophie, Christine, Donovan, Su
Skills: ui/ux, wireframing, user research, prototyping
Tools: Figma, Adobe After Effects, Sketch, OverFlow






Design & Research Process

Problem Space

The current Barbarian website does not showcase recent content. Not only some design layouts are inconsistent, but there also are new sub pages (Foundation and Stranger in Residence) to be added, as well as works.




Goals

To add showcase Stranger in Residence (internal projects where Barbarian collaborate with external experts) and Foundation (Barbarian’s gender equality group) on the Barbarian website.

Foundation has developed into a fully backed organization that works hand-in-hand with the agency’s leadership and HR teams to hold Barbarian up to the highest standards of gender equality.

Stranger In Residence is an invitation to artists, musicians, creative technologists, and experimental thinkers to collaborate on a mutually conceived project at Barbarian.



Primary Research

For our primary research, we decided to do an internal and an external audit for our current site as well as external sites. This will help us understand the structure and point out specific design layouts and contents that need to be adjusted or get inspired by.



Current Site Audit

To start, I did an evaluation of successful areas in web UI of the existing website as well as explored key areas of improvement for Barbarian website in brand, experience, and content. When researching, I focused on the brand (visual), site, and content.

For example, on the current website, there is an inconsistent template layout across the pages, which breaks the overall flow.

Another example includes how the landing home page of the current site has exactly the same works as the projects displayed in the “Work” section. Either the content for the “Work” section need to be updated, or the home page needs something more engaging.



External Site Audit

Additionally, we explored different options of other ad agency’s websites, as well as greatly designed portfolio websites to gain inspiration. We did a deep dive into cover pages, home pages, project/work pages, navigation bar, and culture/about pages.

For example, here is a website that has a landing page for one of the pages that have big categories that fill the whole page. This makes it obvious that the sub pages within this page is more important than the general category itself.

We can apply a design similar to this for our new “Culture” page, since the sub categories (Foundation + SIR) has more content.

One interesting example for the cover page/home page is here, where the navigation bar is hidden at first, but when clicked, it becomes the full screen. This helps the overall pages to display content more clearly, especially if we decide to add additional pages to the navigation bar.



Low Fi Wireframes

We did a several iterations of wireframing in Sketch, for our Culture page, Foundation and SIR pages, as well as a new version of the Work page. We let the culture landing page to be more simple, since the content pages (Foundation + SIR) within the tab is more important in this case.

Key Takeaways

  • During this internship, I learned that it is important to research beforehand, in order to understand the design principles within the brand guidelines

  • I also learned that there needs to be multiple iterations with not only the design team, but also the account and the developers to finalize the website redesign

  • User flows and scenarios are key factors that help the flow of the website to be easily navigated
